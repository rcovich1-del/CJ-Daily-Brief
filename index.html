07Strategy in Focus

Bear Call Spread

Selected for: Defense regime · Tuesday rotation · bearish-to-neutral bias, defined risk, net credit

A bear call spread sells an out-of-the-money call and buys a further out-of-the-money call in the same expiration. It collects a net credit and profits if the underlying stays below the short strike. Maximum loss is the width between strikes less the credit received, so the risk is known the moment the position is opened.

Why it fits today. The regime is defense because SPY closed below its 50-day, and QQQ is a full 3.19% under its own. A bear call spread expresses that without requiring a crash — it wins if price simply fails to rally, which is the base case when a market is capped by broken moving averages. Meanwhile VIX at 18.65 with a contango term structure means calls carry real premium but the market is not pricing a panic, so credits are collectable without paying up for tail protection.

Educational Example — Illustrative Only

Take a hypothetical $100 stock trading below its 50-day moving average, IV rank near 60, no earnings inside 30 days. A trader might build a 30–45 day bear call spread by selling the roughly 25-delta call and buying a call 5 points higher.

  • Short call: ~25 delta, placed above the broken 50-day so the moving average acts as overhead resistance
  • Long call: 5 points higher, defining maximum risk at $500 per spread less the credit
  • Target credit: roughly one-third of the width, so about $1.65 on a 5-point spread
  • Breakeven: the short strike plus the credit received — everything below that is profitable at expiration
  • Probability profile: a 25-delta short call implies roughly a 75% chance of finishing out of the money, before accounting for the credit cushion
  • Management: many traders close at 50% of maximum profit, or roll the spread up and out if the short strike is breached with time remaining

Strikes are expressed in delta and percentage terms deliberately. This describes how the structure works — it is not a recommendation to place any specific trade.

The main risk. Bear call spreads lose on a sharp rally, and this market has a live upside catalyst: chips are rebounding, and a strong Tesla or Alphabet print Wednesday night could gap the index through resistance before there is any chance to adjust. That is exactly why the long wing is not optional, and why single names with earnings inside 14 days were filtered out of the setups above.
