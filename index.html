Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Credit

Bull Put Spread

A Bull Put Spread sells an out-of-the-money put and buys a further-OTM put below it, collecting a net credit that is kept in full if the stock stays above the short strike at expiration.

Best deployed on a stock you are neutral-to-bullish on when implied volatility is elevated, so the premium collected is rich relative to the defined risk.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: > 40% preferred BPS

Why Tonight

With the regime in Caution after Friday's jobs-driven selloff, outright directional risk is unattractive — but the same shock inflated put premium across quality names to extreme levels. A defined-risk bull put spread lets the desk harvest that elevated IV while capping downside: the right tool when you want to lean slightly long without betting on a V-shaped bounce.

VIX 21.51 (+40%) — elevated zone; front-end backwardation means premium is unusually rich for credit sellers.

SPY -2.5% Friday, holding above its 50-day but below the 20-day — choppy, not broken; sell spreads beneath support, not at-the-money.

  • Confirm earnings (est. Jul 21) remain >14 days out so the spread expires before the report.
  • Target ~0.25 delta on the short put for a balanced probability/credit trade.
  • Size so max loss on the $10-wide spread is within risk limits — defined-risk, not risk-free.
  • Plan management up front: take profit near 50% of max credit; roll or close if the short strike is breached.

Generic Structure Example

Sell a put around 0.25 delta / buy a put roughly one strike width (~$10) lower · 30–45 DTE · aim to collect ~25–35% of the spread width in net credit. Illustrative sizing on TXN: short put near $255, long put near $245.
