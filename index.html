Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined-Risk Debit

Ratio Call Diagonal

Buy a longer-dated, in-the-money call and sell one or more shorter-dated, higher-strike calls to finance it - a bullish structure that collects front-month theta while keeping defined, net-long-delta upside.

Best in an uptrend that's grinding higher with moderate (not extreme) implied volatility, so the long call isn't overpriced and the short call still pays.

DTE: Long 60-90 DTE / Short 20-30 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank 30-60% preferred RCD

Why Tonight

With VIX at 16.6, SPY and QQQ at fresh highs above rising 20- and 50-day averages, and 64% of the S&P above its 20-day, the tape favors defined-risk bullish structures. A call diagonal leans long while the sold call harvests still-elevated front-month premium - a fit for a constructive-but-complacent backdrop.

VIX 16.6 (moderate) in contango - the 9-day (13.1) sits well below the 3-month (19.2), calm enough to buy the long call yet rich enough to sell the front.

SPY +0.78% and QQQ +1.70% to record highs, both above rising 20- and 50-day SMAs; internal rotation is violent but the index trend is intact.
