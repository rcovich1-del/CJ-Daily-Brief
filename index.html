NVDA Q1 FY27 — Monster Beat
BULLISH

INTU Post-Earnings Collapse — Intuit −15%
BEARISH
Intuit reported AMC Wednesday and opened Thursday down 15.1% — well beyond the ±9% implied move. Weak small business segment growth and a cut to FY guidance spooked the market. Spreads positioned for a contained move took max loss. This is the week's clearest reminder: binary events blow through spread widths regardless of setup quality. Earnings date verification is non-negotiable.
WMT Earnings — Technical Beat, Investor Disappointment
NEUTRAL
Walmart reported EPS $0.66 vs $0.65 estimate — a narrow beat — but stock fell 7%+ on cautious consumer commentary and tariff-related cost pressure guidance. Retail sector broadly soft Thursday. COST, TGT, and TJX on follow-on watch next week. The WMT reaction confirms that earnings quality and guidance tone matter far more than headline EPS in the current environment.
Bond Yields Fall — Rate Relief Supports Equities
BULLISH
10-year Treasury yield pulled back toward 4.35% as softer-than-expected economic data reinforced the Fed's hold-steady narrative. Lower yields are broadly supportive of equity valuations, particularly for rate-sensitive sectors and growth names. The yield move is adding a constructive tailwind to the NVDA-driven tech rally and giving real estate and utilities some relief.
Fed Watch & Trade Policy — US-China Truce, July Expiry Risk
WATCH
Fed officials remain in data-dependency mode with June cut probability at ~25% per Fed Funds futures. The more impactful near-term factor: the US-China 90-day tariff pause continues to provide a constructive risk backdrop — but its July expiry is a known tail risk that options desks are quietly beginning to price. Meanwhile, US-Iran nuclear negotiations could ease energy supply concerns. Watch Powell on Tuesday for any tone shift. Regime: Transitional — elevated uncertainty but improving momentum.

Friday brings a constructive close to a tech-dominated week. NVDA's monster quarter was the catalyst the market needed to shake off the April tariff malaise — the follow-through into semis, mega-cap growth, and the broader tech complex is real and broadening. When IV finally compresses, the premium collected today looks prescient in retrospect.

Two things from this week deserve weight. First, NVDA's beat is more than an earnings event — it's a confirmation that the AI infrastructure cycle is in year two of a multi-year buildout. The implications for semis, data infrastructure, and enterprise software are durable. Second, INTU's 15% gap-down on a "contained" setup is the week's most important lesson: no matter how clean a credit spread looks on the screener, a binary event can and will blow through your width. Earnings calendar discipline is not optional — it is the highest-ROI habit in this trade style.

Heading into a 3-day weekend, the posture is selective: two high-conviction entries (ENPH, QCOM) sized at 50% for any Friday execution, with full sizing from Tuesday's open. The trend is constructive, the setup is rich, and the right move is to be patient and precise — not to chase the week's momentum into a weekend gap risk.
