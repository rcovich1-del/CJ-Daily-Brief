Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Undefined Risk Credit

Cash Secured Put

A Cash Secured Put sells a put option below the current stock price while holding cash equal to the obligation to purchase 100 shares — generating income while expressing a bullish or neutral view on the underlying.

Ideal when IV Rank is elevated (>40%), you are willing to own the stock at the strike price, the stock is in an uptrend or neutral range, and there are no earnings within the trade window.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank > 40% preferred CSP

Why Tonight

Tonight's Normal regime (VIX 15.77, SPY above 20-day SMA) is a textbook CSP environment. Implied volatility is elevated enough to generate meaningful premium, but not so high as to signal systemic risk that could impair the underlying. The pullback in electronic components today creates attractive entry points for premium sellers who are comfortable with ownership at a discount.

VIX at 15.77 — Moderate Zone. IV levels are elevated enough for solid CSP credits without signaling a regime-level risk event.

SPY above 20-day SMA ($744.83) — trend intact. Today's pullback is geopolitical noise, not a trend break. Bullish structures remain appropriate.

  • Review price chart for $53–55 support structure — is this a prior base or just a round number? Check for recent price action confirmation

  • Review sector context — electronic components are exposed to global macro and tariff risk. Any overnight supply-chain news or China trade developments?
  • Calculate break-even at expiration: if premium collected = $1.80 at $55 strike, effective cost basis = $53.20. Is this a price at which you'd want to own 100 shares?
  • Confirm max loss tolerance: selling $55 CSP with $1.80 credit = max loss of $5,320 per contract if VSH goes to zero. Does this fit your position-sizing rules (1–3% of account)?

Generic Structure Example

For a CSP on a $60–65 stock: Sell a put at approximately 85–90% of current price (~30 delta) · 30–45 DTE · Target credit of at least 2–3% of the strike price · Maximum loss = (Strike – Credit Received) × 100 · If assigned, effective purchase price = Strike – Premium Collected
