Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Credit

Bull Put Spread

A Bull Put Spread sells an out-of-the-money put and buys a further out-of-the-money put below it, collecting a net credit that is kept in full if the stock holds above the short strike at expiration.

Best in elevated-IV-rank names with a neutral-to-up trend, where you want defined risk, a high probability of profit, and time decay working in your favor.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank > 40% preferred BPS

Why Tonight

Tonight's tape is mixed: VIX is benign at 17.6 and two-thirds of the S&P sit above their 20-day, but the index slipped back to that average as semis sold off. A Bull Put Spread fits this caution regime because it is defined-risk, profits from elevated implied volatility, and only needs the underlying to hold support rather than rally — and we apply it to a relative-strength leader, not a falling semiconductor.

VIX 17.65 (−2.5%) sits in the moderate zone; IV ranks are elevated across the tape, making premium-selling attractive while the term structure stays in contango (VXST 15.5 < VXV 19.5).

SPY −0.7% back to its 20-day and QQQ −1.4% on semi weakness, but IWM +0.3% and breadth firm — a rotation, not a breakdown.
