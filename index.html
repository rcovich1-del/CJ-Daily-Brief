Macro & News
KEY DRIVERS
Global Tech & Semiconductor Rout
HIGH
An accelerating global selloff in AI and semiconductor names dragged U.S. equities lower. South Korea’s Kospi plunged ~10% (triggering a halt), Samsung and SK Hynix each fell more than 12%, and Japan’s Nikkei dropped 3.6%. QQQ shed roughly 2.8% on the week as investors de-risk high-multiple, AI-capex exposure.
Hawkish Fed Repricing
HIGH
A more hawkish tone from Fed Chair Kevin Warsh — flagging a potential rate hike as soon as September alongside firmer data — compressed equity risk premia. VIX jumped to 19.49 (+18.8% on the week) and the front of the vol curve steepened sharply.
Micron (MU) Reports Wednesday
MEDIUM
Micron reports June 24 after the close and is shaping up as the next catalyst for the AI/memory complex. Semiconductor IV is pinned near 1-year highs — ENTG, KLAC, ON, TSM and a dozen peers sit at ~100% IV Rank — so premium is rich but event risk is elevated through the print.
U.S. Flash PMI in Focus
MEDIUM
Markets parsed S&P Global flash PMI data amid the risk-off tape; firmer activity readings reinforced the hawkish Fed narrative. Oil eased on signs of geopolitical progress, including potential U.S. sanction waivers on Iranian crude — a disinflationary offset to the growth scare.
Regime: Caution — Rotation Underway
INFO
VIX 19.49 (Moderate) with the term structure flattening (VXST 19.5 vs 3-mo VXV 21.1) signals rising near-term risk. Breadth softened — 53.7% of S&P 500 names hold above their 20-day and 58.8% above the 50-day. Leadership rotated hard into value and defensives (Real Estate, Energy, Financials, Utilities) and down-cap (IWM +0.9%), while mega-cap tech and Communication Services led the tape lower. SPY is below its 20-day but still holding the 50-day — a constructive-but-fragile backdrop that rewards patience and defined risk.

Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Undefined Risk Credit

Cash Secured Put

Sell a put on a stock you’d be happy to own, collecting premium upfront in exchange for the obligation to buy 100 shares at the strike if assigned.

Best in elevated-IV, range-to-bullish conditions on quality names you’re willing to accumulate at a discount.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank > 40% preferred CSP

Why Tonight

With the regime in Caution and VIX near 20, IV Rank is elevated across the board — ideal conditions to get paid for selling premium. But a sharp risk-off tape and Micron’s pending print argue for choosing resilient names and keeping strikes well below support.

VIX 19.49 (+3.0%, Moderate Zone) — rich enough premium to make put-selling attractive, but the term structure is flattening (VXST 19.5 vs 3-mo VXV 21.1), a sign near-term risk is creeping up.

SPY 733.58 sits below its 20-day but still holds the 50-day; QQQ broke down 0.4% as money rotated into value, financials and small-caps. Favor put-selling on relative strength, not on the falling knives.
