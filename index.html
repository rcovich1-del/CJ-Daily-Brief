Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Bull Call Diagonal

A bull diagonal buys a longer-dated, deeper in-the-money call and sells a shorter-dated, out-of-the-money call against it - a capital-efficient, directionally bullish structure that finances the long call with recurring short-call premium.

Best when you are moderately bullish on a name holding above support with moderate IV, and want defined risk plus theta offset from the short leg.

DTE: 30-60 DTE (long leg) IV Rank: IV Rank 30-50% preferred Bull Diagonal

Why Tonight

Tonight's caution regime - VIX 18.4, the S&P below its 20-day but holding the 50-day - argues against naked premium selling and against aggressive long-call debits. A bull diagonal threads that needle: defined risk, a long-vega back-month leg that benefits if vol stays bid, and a short front-month call that cushions a choppy, headline-driven tape. Financials led today on the hawkish-rate read, a constructive backdrop for select bullish diagonals.

VIX 18.44 (+1.3%) - moderate zone; the back-month long call benefits from any further vol expansion.

SPY -0.6% below its 20-day but +1.8% above the 50-day; QQQ -1.9% as semis lead lower - favor non-tech diagonals.

  • Compare IV Rank (39%) vs IV %ile (55%) - the back-month long call is reasonably priced, not rich.
  • Map the short-call strike to overhead resistance near $240 to maximize credit without capping upside too early.
  • Stress the position for a semis-led pullback - NTRA is diagnostics/healthcare, lower correlation to today's tech weakness.
  • Confirm liquidity - NTRA traded ~2,471 contracts (RelVol 1.26); use limit orders given wider biotech spreads.
  • Size for defined max loss = net debit; predefine a mental stop if $215 breaks on volume.

Generic Structure Example

Buy a back-month call ~70 delta (30-60 DTE) and sell a front-month call ~30 delta (7-21 DTE) against it. Roll the short call each cycle to reduce cost basis while the long leg carries the directional exposure.
