Macro & News
KEY DRIVERS
10-Year Treasury Hits 1-Year High Above 4.60%
HIGH
The 10Y Treasury yield rose to its highest level in over a year today, breaching 4.60% on the back of two consecutive disappointing inflation reports last week. Rising rates pressured growth and rate-sensitive sectors — tech fell -1.61%, real estate -1.56%. Rate cuts under new Fed Chair Warsh appear increasingly distant.
Dominion Energy Surges 14%+ on NextEra Merger Reports
HIGH
Shares of Dominion Energy (D) surged ~14.3% on reports of acquisition discussions with NextEra Energy (NEE). A deal would create the largest utility company in the US. Energy sector was the only green sector today (+2.32%), partially amplified by this consolidation catalyst.
Crude Oil Spikes +4.20% to $108.66 — Iran Conflict Risk
MEDIUM
WTI crude surged 4.2% to $108.66/bbl on escalating geopolitical risk tied to the Iran conflict. Supply disruption fears are driving oil premium. This adds to inflation persistence concerns and reinforces the Fed's case for holding rates. Energy sector outperformed on the week +5.89%.
REGN -11.5% — Earnings Miss on Higher Expenses
MEDIUM
Regeneron (REGN) fell -11.5% after reporting Q1 EPS of $6.99 (vs. ~$7.90 consensus), below prior year's $7.58, as operating expenses rose sharply despite revenue rising 19% YoY to $3.61B. Eylea HD delivered $468M in US sales (+52% YoY) but couldn't offset the margin compression. Healthcare sector fell -1.08%.
Fed Transition: Warsh Confirmed — April FOMC Minutes Drop Wednesday
INFO
Kevin Warsh was confirmed as the new Fed Chair by the Senate last Wednesday (May 13), succeeding Jerome Powell. The April FOMC meeting minutes — Powell's final meeting — release Wednesday at 2 PM ET. The Fed held rates at 3.50–3.75% at that meeting, with three FOMC members dissenting due to hawkish inflation concerns. Market expects no rate cuts in the near term as services inflation, tariffs, and energy prices remain elevated. NVDA earnings also Wednesday — a double catalyst for market direction.
