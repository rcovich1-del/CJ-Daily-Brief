Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Ratio Call Diagonal

A Ratio Call Diagonal buys a longer-dated in-the-money call and sells a greater number of shorter-dated out-of-the-money calls against it, financing upside exposure with front-month theta.

Best when moderately bullish on a name with a gently rising trend and mid-range implied volatility that lets you sell rich front-month premium.

DTE: Back-month 45–60 DTE / front-month 7–14 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank 40–60% preferred RCD

Why Tonight

With the VIX at 15.7 and the S&P holding above its 20- and 50-day averages, the tape is constructive enough to lean bullish, while mid-range single-name IV Ranks (40–60%) make selling the front-month leg worthwhile. Moderate breadth argues for defined-risk directional structures like the RCD over naked long calls.

VIX 15.67 (-3.9%) with the 9-day VIX at 12.1 — low near-term fear supports holding a longer-dated long call while selling cheap-to-moderate front-month premium.

SPY +0.4% to $754.81 and QQQ +1.1% to $717.74, both above their 20- and 50-day averages — a gently rising backdrop that suits a bullish diagonal.
