Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Credit

Bull Put Spread

Sell an out-of-the-money put and buy a further out-of-the-money put in the same expiration, collecting a net credit that you keep in full if the stock stays above your short strike.

Best when implied volatility is rich relative to its own history and the underlying is holding above a support level you can defend — you get paid for a move that does not have to happen.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank > 40% preferred BPS

Why Tonight

Monday in a Normal regime is the desk's rotation slot for the Bull Put Spread, and tonight the data cooperates — with one caveat. VIX at 17.16 is elevated enough to fatten put premium but still below the 18 line, and SPY holds above its 20-day, so the mechanical regime test passes. What has changed is where a bullish structure is defensible: with Info Tech -2.07% and Energy +3.16%, the strategy is right but the sector selection carries all the risk.

VIX 17.16 (+14.17%) — the sweet spot for credit spreads. Rich enough to pay you properly, low enough that you are not selling into a panic. The 9-day VIX at 15.13 remains below the 3-month at 19.64, so the curve is in contango and there is no scramble for near-dated protection.

SPY -0.77% but still 0.64% above its 20-day and 0.97% above its 50-day; QQQ -1.90% and now 1.46% BELOW its 20-day. The index-level uptrend is intact, but the leadership underneath it has flipped from growth to value.

Tonight's Research Checklist

  • Confirm the Jul 28 earnings date on Market Chameleon — a 38 DTE spread expires AFTER the print, so either shorten DTE to stay clear or accept the event risk consciously.
  • RSI(14) is 73.3 — overbought. Decide whether you are selling a put into strength or chasing a 4.7% up-day; a pullback toward $78–79 gives a far better entry.
  • Verify $78.81 (20-day MA) has actually held as support on the daily chart, not just as a number — check how price behaved there on the last two touches.
  • Stress the thesis against a Hormuz de-escalation headline. Crude gave DINO its +4.7%; a ceasefire tweet takes it back. What does the spread look like if WTI gives back 5%?
  • Check the credit relative to width — a $5-wide spread should pay at least $0.65 (13% of width) or the risk/reward is not worth the margin.
  • IV Rank is 92% but absolute IV is 49.9% — confirm IV/HV is above 1.0 so you are genuinely selling overpriced vol, not just vol that is high because realized vol is high.
  • Options volume was 10,890 with RelVol 1.16 — check the actual bid/ask on the $78/$73 spread before assuming you can get filled near the mid.

Generic Structure Example

Sell a put around 30 delta / buy a put around 15 delta, 30–45 DTE, targeting a credit of at least 13% of the strike width, with the short strike below the 20-day moving average and outside the 1σ expected move.
