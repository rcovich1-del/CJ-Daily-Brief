Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Bull Call Diagonal

A bull call diagonal buys a longer-dated, deeper call and sells a shorter-dated, higher-strike call to finance it - a defined-risk, capital-efficient way to play steady upside.

Best when the trend is up and implied volatility is low-to-moderate, so the long leg isn't overpriced.

DTE: Front 30-45 / Back 60-90 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank < 50% preferred Bull Diagonal

Why Tonight

Tonight's tape fits the strategy cleanly: VIX at 17.68 keeps long-dated calls affordable, SPY and QQQ are trending higher, and a breadth thrust (71% of the S&P above its 20-day) argues for continuation rather than mean reversion. A diagonal stays long delta while the short front-month call harvests theta.

VIX at 17.68 (-12.5%) sits in the moderate zone - low enough that the long-dated call's vega isn't richly priced.

SPY +1.70% and QQQ +3.38%, with small caps (IWM +3.0%) breaking out above their 20-day average.
