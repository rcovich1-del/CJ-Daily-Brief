Strategy in Focus
Neutral · Defined Risk Credit

Iron Condor

A four-leg, market-neutral credit strategy that simultaneously sells an out-of-the-money put spread and call spread, profiting when the underlying stays range-bound and implied volatility contracts.

Best deployed on liquid, range-bound names with elevated IV Rank and no binary event risk before expiration.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank > 40% preferred IC

Why Tonight

Tonight’s caution regime — VIX at 19.4 in the moderate 18-22 band with SPY back under its 20-day — is tailor-made for a defined-risk neutral structure. Today’s 12% VIX pop richened premium across the board, and with the tape rotating rather than trending cleanly, selling both wings collects that inflated vol while capping risk on each side.

VIX 19.4 (+11.8%) — elevated enough to fatten condor credits, not so high that a directional break is the base case.

SPY −1.6% and QQQ −2.0% with defensives bid signals rotation, not a one-way trend — favorable for range-bound premium selling.

Tonight's Research Checklist

  • Confirm AFL has no earnings before expiration — last report was late April, next is expected late July (cleared for a 30–45 DTE condor).
  • Map the ±$1.81 (±1.6%) expected move and set short strikes outside it — roughly $113 put / $120 call as a starting frame.
  • Verify IV Rank (46.5%) is still above 40% at entry; if today’s vol spike fades fast, the credit shrinks.
  • Locate support near the 50-day (~$114) and resistance below the recent high to define the range.
  • Check option liquidity — AFL traded ~1,600 contracts today; use limit orders and watch bid/ask width on all four legs.
  • Size so max loss on either spread is a small, fixed fraction of the book.
  • Pre-define management: take profit near ~50% of max credit, or roll the tested side if price nears a short strike.

Generic Structure Example

Sell a put ~20–25 delta and a call ~20–25 delta, buy protective wings ~10 delta on each side · 30–45 DTE · target net credit ≥ one-third of the wing width.
