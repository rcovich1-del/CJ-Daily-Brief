Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Long Call

A long call buys the right to purchase 100 shares at a set strike, delivering leveraged, defined-risk exposure to an upside move.

Best when you expect a move higher and implied volatility is relatively low, so you are not overpaying for the option premium.

DTE: 30-45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank < 50% preferred LC

Why Tonight

Tonight's tape is textbook for a debit-call setup: a risk-on rally led by mega-cap tech and semis, the Dow at a record, and VIX compressing to ~16. With the regime classified Normal and rotation favoring offense, buying defined-risk upside in a lower-IV name lets you participate without selling cheap premium.

VIX 16.41 (-8.4%) — low/moderate zone; a contango term structure keeps call time-decay manageable.

SPY +1.8% and above its 20-day, with QQQ leadership intact — the trend supports long-delta exposure.
