Cash Secured Put (CSP)

A cash secured put involves selling an OTM put option against full cash collateral equal to the obligation. The seller collects premium upfront and assumes the obligation to purchase 100 shares at the strike if the stock closes below it at expiration.

Deploy in normal-to-bullish regimes (VIX < 20) when IVR ≥ 40% and you are comfortable owning the underlying at the strike price. High IVR inflates put premiums — CSP sellers collect above-average credit for the same strike distance.

DTE: 21–45 DTE IV Rank: IVR ≥ 40% SELL PUT

Why Tonight

Tonight's conditions are textbook for CSP: VIX 16.29 (moderate, not extreme), SPY at all-time highs above all SMAs, normal regime intact. Elevated IVR across the options desk means sellers collect fat premium with a defined-risk P&L profile. Thursday rotation into CSP aligns with the CJ Capital weekly playbook.

VIX 16.29 — Moderate zone (15–20). IVR elevated on multiple names; premium is rich relative to realized vol.

SPY at ATH, Nasdaq at ATH, Russell +0.70%. Uptrend intact across all timeframes. CSP short strikes placed well below current price have structural support.
