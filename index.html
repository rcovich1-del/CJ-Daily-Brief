Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Bull Call Diagonal

Buy a longer-dated in-the-money call and sell a shorter-dated out-of-the-money call against it, financing the long leg while staying net bullish.

Best when you want defined-risk upside exposure with a defensive, income-cushioned structure — ideal in a choppy, mixed-signal tape.

DTE: Long leg 45–60 DTE / short leg 7–15 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank 20–40% preferred (buy time, sell elevated front) Bull Diagonal

Why Tonight

Tonight’s Caution regime (VIX 16.9, mixed breadth, tech under pressure but SPY still above its 20-day) argues against naked directional debit. A bull diagonal keeps a bullish tilt while the sold front-month call cushions theta and vega if the chop continues — a fitting way to lean long on a resilient, defensive sector.

VIX 16.9 (+3.6%) sits in the moderate zone; front-month IV is elevated enough to make the short call worthwhile without crushing the long leg.

SPY -0.48% but still +0.5% above its 20-day; QQQ -1.85% and below its 20-day — leadership is rotating toward defensives like Real Estate (+1.5%).
