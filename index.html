Bullish · Defined Risk Credit (cash-secured)

Cash Secured Put

Sell an out-of-the-money put fully backed by cash — you are paid up front to commit to buying shares at a discount to today's price.

Bullish-to-neutral on a name you'd genuinely own, when implied volatility is elevated and price holds above defined support.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank ≥ 40% preferred CSP

Why Tonight

VIX at 15.2 with SPY 1.5% above its 20-day keeps the regime normal, and Thursday's session rotated leadership, not risk — 60.6% of S&P names hold their 20-day and the NYSE A/D printed +837. That is the backdrop where cash-secured puts work: collect elevated single-name premium while the index floor holds.

VIX 15.23 (-5.2% today) — index vol is cheap, but single-name IV is bifurcated: tonight's example carries a 100% IV Rank at 130% implied. The edge is name selection, not index exposure.

SPY +1.5% and QQQ +2.7% above their 20-day MAs; IWM reclaimed its 20-day with a +1.6% session — the uptrend is intact beneath the semiconductor noise.
