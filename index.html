Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Ratio Call Diagonal

A two-legged diagonal spread that purchases a longer-dated in-the-money call while selling a shorter-dated out-of-the-money call against it, at a ratio that reduces net debit.

Ideal when IV Rank is moderate (40–70%), the stock has a bullish trend, and the trader wants leveraged upside exposure with reduced cost basis versus outright long calls.

DTE: 45–60 DTE back month / 21–30 DTE front IV Rank: IV Rank 40–70% preferred RCD

Why Tonight

Wednesday's normal regime — VIX at 16.29, SPY trending 2.06% above its 20-day MA — is textbook RCD territory. The strategy thrives in low-to-moderate IV environments with a directional bullish bias, where buying back-month calls is affordable and front-month premium collection offsets the debit. With breadth above 55% on both 20D and 50D MAs, the trend is confirmed.

VIX 16.29 — Moderate Zone. Low enough to keep long-option premium reasonable; high enough that front-month calls have meaningful premium to sell.

SPY at $750.46, +2.06% above 20D MA, +7.20% above 50D MA. QQQ and IWM also well above their key MAs. Broad trend confirmation supports directional long exposure.
