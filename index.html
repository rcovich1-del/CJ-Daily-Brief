Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Ratio Call Diagonal

A ratio call diagonal buys one longer-dated call and sells a larger quantity of shorter-dated, higher-strike calls — financing long upside exposure while harvesting front-month theta.

Best in a moderately bullish, low-to-moderate-volatility uptrend where you want cheap long delta but expect a measured grind higher rather than a violent gap.

DTE: Back 30–60 / Front 7–21 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank 40–60% preferred RCD

Why Tonight

Tonight's caution regime — VIX low but breadth thin and the Fed path unresolved — rewards structures that lean long without paying up for naked premium. The RCD funds a back-month call by selling rich, fast-decaying front-month calls, which is exactly what a steep VIX contango makes attractive.

VIX 16.78 with the 9-day (13.93) well below the 3-month (19.57) — calm near-term vol makes selling the front-month calls efficient while the long back-month call stays comparatively cheap.

SPY is holding its 20-day and QQQ sits +1.9% above it; Industrials lead on both a 1-day and 1-week basis — a constructive backdrop for measured-upside diagonals.

  • Map a realistic upside target inside the ±$43 (±14.6%) expected move; the short strikes should sit near it.
  • Check option liquidity at the chosen strikes — bid/ask width and open interest on the 3,183-contract daily volume base.
  • Stress the position for a gap up: model max loss if MOD blows through the short calls before front-month expiry.
  • Pre-define a roll/adjust trigger for the short calls and confirm RSI (~60) isn't yet overbought (>70).

Generic Structure Example

Buy 1 back-month call ~60–70 delta (30–60 DTE) and sell 2 front-month calls ~25–30 delta (7–21 DTE) for a net debit; roll the short calls each cycle to keep financing the long. Defined risk, capped upside.
