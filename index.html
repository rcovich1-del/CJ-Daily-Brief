Macro & News
KEY DRIVERS
Import Prices Surge on Iran Energy Shock
HIGH
April import prices surged 1.9% — the largest monthly increase in four years — far exceeding the 1.0% consensus estimate. Energy costs tied to the Iran conflict and Strait of Hormuz blockade are the primary driver. Year-over-year import prices jumped 4.2%, extending a third straight week of hot inflation data following CPI and PPI prints.
Trump-Xi Summit: Hormuz Deal, Boeing Orders
MEDIUM
President Trump and China's Xi Jinping agreed in Beijing that the Strait of Hormuz must remain a free waterway, with China pledging influence over Iran's decision-making. Treasury Secretary Bessent signaled imminent large Boeing aircraft orders from China as a summit outcome — a constructive catalyst for Industrials and Aerospace.
Cisco Q3 Beat + POET Deal + NVDA H200 Unlock
MEDIUM
Cisco surged 17% after fiscal Q3 revenue rose 12% YoY to $15.8B with EPS of $1.06 beating $1.04 consensus and guidance set ~$1B above the Street. POET Technologies jumped 28% on a $500M+ Lumilens optical-networking supply agreement. The U.S. cleared Nvidia H200 chip sales to 10 Chinese firms — Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance, and JD.com among them.
Retail Sales Miss; Jobless Claims Drift Higher
MEDIUM
April retail sales rose 0.5%, below the 0.6% estimate and sharply down from March's 1.6% surge. Initial jobless claims rose 12K to 211K, above the 205K forecast. Consumer momentum is softening under inflation pressure, though markets chose to focus on earnings beats and trade headlines rather than the soft demand signals.
Regime Context — Records Despite Hot Inflation; VIX Declining
INFO
The S&P 500 closed above 7,500 for the first time in history, with the Dow retaking 50,000 and the Nasdaq at all-time highs — even as three consecutive hot inflation prints (CPI, PPI, Import Prices) raise Fed rate cut uncertainty. VIX declined to 17.29, remaining in the Moderate Zone. The market is pricing in trade tailwinds over inflation headwinds. Regime reads Normal — SPY above all key SMAs, VIX below 18 — supporting a selective credit-selling posture. Primary risk: geopolitical escalation in the Strait of Hormuz.
