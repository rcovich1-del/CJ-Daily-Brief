Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk · Credit

Bull Put Spread

Sell an OTM put spread to collect premium when you hold a mildly bullish to neutral outlook. Max profit = credit received; max loss = spread width − credit. Breakeven = short strike − net credit.

Deploy when IVR > 40%, the underlying is above key moving averages, and you want defined downside risk while selling inflated premium into a sideways-to-up tape.

DTE: 30–45 IV Rank: > 40% Credit Spread · BPS

Why Tonight

Normal regime (VIX < 18, SPY +1.92% above 20D MA) is the ideal BPS environment. Elevated IVR across semiconductors this week provides above-average credit for comparable strikes.

VIX 16.81 — Moderate Zone. Sufficient implied vol to generate meaningful credit without outsized headline risk. VIX -9.39% on the week suggests further compression possible.

SPY RSI 68.78; QQQ RSI 71.37. Trend is intact and breadth is supportive (57.56% of S&P 500 names above 20D MA). Bullish backdrop validates selling puts below support.
