Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Credit

Bull Put Spread

A defined-risk credit spread that sells an out-of-the-money put and buys a lower-strike put at the same expiration, collecting net premium while capping maximum risk to the spread width minus credit received.

Best deployed when the underlying is neutral-to-bullish, IV Rank is elevated above 40%, and the short put strike is placed below key technical support levels.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank > 40% BPS

Why Tonight

Tonight's Normal regime (VIX 16.05, SPY +2.34% above 20D MA) combined with an active AI earnings cycle creates ideal BPS conditions. Elevated IVR across tech names offers rich premium while the bullish macro backdrop keeps directional risk manageable. Bull Put Spread is the Monday strategy in Normal regime — the screener delivered 15+ qualifying candidates tonight.

VIX 16.05 — Moderate Zone, declining -2.67% today. Term structure in contango (VXST 13.76 < VXV 19.43 < VXMT 22.13). Near-term fear is subdued — credit collection is efficient, especially in names where stock-specific IV has spiked from earnings.

SPY at new ATH ($758.54), QQQ above 27,000 for the first time. Both indices trending strongly higher and above all key moving averages. Bullish directional backdrop supports put spread placement well below current prices.

Generic Structure Example

This produces a credit approximately 30–40% of the spread width.
