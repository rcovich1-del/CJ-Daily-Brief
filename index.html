Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Long Call

A long call buys a single call option for leveraged, defined-risk upside — the most you can lose is the premium paid, while upside above the strike is theoretically unlimited.

Best when you expect a directional move higher and implied volatility is low-to-moderate, so the premium is cheap relative to the move you're paying for.

DTE: 30-45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank < 50% preferred LC

Why Tonight

Tonight is a normal regime — VIX 16.5 and both SPY and QQQ trending above rising 20-day averages. In a calm, trending tape, paying for defined-risk directional exposure is reasonable versus selling premium at compressed index vol. Note tonight's featured example carries mid-range single-stock IV, so vega drag is a live consideration.

VIX 16.45 (-4.1%), moderate zone — low enough that broad call premium isn't overly inflated.

SPY +1.65% / QQQ +2.49%, both above rising 20-day averages — trend structure supports directional longs.

  • Size so the premium-at-risk fits your per-trade budget — a long call can expire worthless.
