Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Long Call

A long call buys the right to purchase stock at a set strike, giving leveraged upside exposure with risk capped at the premium paid.

Best when you expect a directional move higher and implied volatility is low-to-moderate, so you are not overpaying for premium.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank < 50% preferred LC

Why Tonight

With the VIX easing to 16.1 and the S&P holding above its 20- and 50-day averages, the backdrop favors defined-risk directional longs. Low outright IV in the trending names makes buying calls cheaper than fading strength, and Tuesday's semiconductor rebound argues for leaning into quality uptrends rather than shorting them. On the normal-regime Tuesday rotation, the Long Call is the featured offensive structure.

VIX 16.1 (moderate) with a contango term structure — relatively cheap optionality for debit buyers.

SPY +0.87% and holding above its 20/50-day; QQQ +1.43% rebounding though still just under its 20-day.
