Macro & News
KEY DRIVERS
Fed Chair Transition: Powell Out, Warsh In
Hawkish Shift
Jerome Powell's term expired May 15. Kevin Warsh is confirmed as the new Federal Reserve Chair, signaling reduced forward guidance, fewer press conferences, and a less accommodative posture. Markets are repricing the policy trajectory with 10-year yields spiking 9bps to 4.55% — a 12-month high.
Rate Hike Probability Jumps to 45%
Yield Risk
CME FedWatch now prices a 45% probability of at least one rate hike in 2026, up from just 1% one month ago. Three April FOMC voters dissented against cut-leaning statement language. Goolsbee, Kashkari, and Logan all flagged persistent inflation pressures — the minutes drop Wednesday.
March CPI Reaccelerates to 3.3% YoY
Inflation Risk
Headline CPI rose 3.3% year-over-year in March (vs. 2.4% in February), with a 0.9% month-over-month print driven by a 21.2% surge in gasoline prices. Energy sector's +6.75% weekly gain reflects the commodity push. Core inflation is sticky, limiting Fed flexibility.
Empire State Manufacturing Beats Hard: +19.6
Growth Signal
May Empire State Manufacturing Index jumped to 19.6 vs. the 6.2 consensus (prev: 11.0) — the strongest reading in several months. Industrial demand is re-accelerating, consistent with the oil surge. Industrials and Materials should be on radar for follow-through if this holds.
Breadth Divergence Warning — Tech Masks Broad Weakness
Internals Warning
Friday's headline gains (+0.79% SPY, +0.71% QQQ) were almost entirely driven by mega-cap tech (XLK +1.85%). Beneath the surface: NYSE Advance-Decline was -930, only 35.2% of S&P 500 names trade above their 20-day MA, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average closed DOWN -1.03%. Seven of eleven sectors ended flat or negative. This breadth divergence is a late-cycle warning signal — rallies built on narrow leadership tend to be fragile. Maintain defined-risk structures, reduce allocation toward full book, and watch the ADDN trend into next week.

Desk Take
CLOSING NOTE
Selective
Friday, May 15, 2026

Friday delivered the classic late-bull setup: headline indices green, breadth red. SPY +0.79%, QQQ +0.71% — scoreboard looks fine. But beneath it, NYSE printed -930 advance-decline, only 35% of S&P names are above their 20-day MA, and the Dow Industrial actually closed down 1.03%. Technology carried the entire session. Every other sector was a coin flip at best.

The bigger story is the regime shift happening at the Fed. Jerome Powell's term expired Friday and Kevin Warsh steps in with a distinctly hawkish posture — less transparency, less guidance, less tolerance for inflation. With the 10-year yield at a 12-month high of 4.55%, rate hike probabilities jumping from 1% to 45% in a single month, and March CPI printing a hot 3.3%, the macro backdrop has quietly shifted from accommodative to cautious. We are not in a clean bull regime.

For the options desk, VIX at 18.43 keeps us in Selective mode — premium exists, but only for the disciplined. PANW, CSCO, and AMAT all cleared earnings this week and sit with elevated IVR. These are the spots: event risk behind, IV still elevated, defined-risk BPS structures appropriate. Wednesday's FOMC minutes are the wildcard — three dissenters in Powell's final meeting could confirm the hawkish pivot in writing and send VIX back above 20 fast. Size accordingly before the print.

Top Focus
FOMC Minutes — Wed May 20, 2:00 PM ET. Three dissenters at April meeting + new hawkish chair = material risk of VIX spike. Reduce new entries Mon-Tue; let the minutes clear before adding exposure.
