KEY DRIVERS
U.S.-Iran Interim Deal Sinks Crude
HIGH
The U.S. and Iran reached an interim peace deal that would reopen the Strait of Hormuz, sending crude down more than 5%. Pakistan's PM said the agreement will be signed Friday in Switzerland, with Qatar hosting delegations this week. Treasuries rallied across the curve as lower oil eased inflation concerns.
FOMC Wednesday - Warsh's First Meeting
HIGH
The Fed convenes June 16-17, the first meeting chaired by new Chair Kevin Warsh. A hold is widely expected, but markets watch for removal of the 'easing bias' and a more hawkish, hike-or-cut-neutral signal. Updated projections and the dot plot land Wednesday at 2:00 PM, with the press conference at 2:30 PM.
Fox to Acquire Roku; FOXA -13%
MEDIUM
Fox Corporation announced an agreement to acquire Roku, and the acquirer's shares cratered - FOXA -13% and FOX -10% - on deal dilution and integration concerns. Roku jumped on the bid. The headline drove outsized options activity (FOX RelVol ~4.9x) and pushed Fox into the bearish-diagonal screen.
Semis Surge, Miners Rally
MEDIUM
Chips led the tape - MU +8%, LRCX +5%, AMAT +4.8% - and dominate tonight's high-IV premium-selling screens. Gold and silver miners rallied (NEM +4.8%, SLV +4.4%) as rates and the dollar eased. Breadth was narrower than the indexes suggest, with NYSE advancers trailing (A/D -211).
Regime: Low-Vol but Event-Gated
INFO

Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Credit

Bull Put Spread

Sell an out-of-the-money put and buy a further OTM put to collect net credit with defined downside risk.

Best in a stable-to-rising market with elevated implied volatility and the stock holding above support.

DTE: 30-45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank > 40% preferred BPS

Why Tonight

With VIX at 16.2, SPY above its 20-day, and breadth healthy (69.6% of the S&P above the 20-day), the backdrop favors selling downside premium. Today's normal regime and Monday rotation put the Bull Put Spread in focus, and semiconductor leadership gives bullish put spreads a strong thematic tailwind. The one check: keep expiration past or sized for Wednesday's FOMC.

VIX 16.2 (-9%) with a contango term structure (VXST 15.6 < VXV 19.4) - calm at the index level, but single-name IV ranks are extreme, the sweet spot for credit spreads.

SPY +0.5% and QQQ +0.6%, both above all major moving averages; semiconductors lead.
