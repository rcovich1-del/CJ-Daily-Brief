Strategy in Focus
Neutral · Defined Risk Credit

Iron Condor

A four-leg, market-neutral credit strategy that simultaneously sells an out-of-the-money put spread and call spread, profiting when the underlying stays range-bound and implied volatility contracts.

Best deployed on liquid, range-bound names with elevated IV Rank and no earnings inside the expiration cycle.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank > 40% preferred IC

Why Tonight

Tonight's caution regime — VIX in the 18–22 band with the S&P pinned just below its 20-day but holding the 50-day — is tailor-made for a neutral, premium-selling structure. Elevated implied vol inflates the credit while two-sided, range-bound price action rewards strategies that profit from time decay rather than direction.

VIX 18.89 (-4.4%) — moderate zone; term structure in contango (VXST 17.9 < VXV 20.3) signals no acute near-term stress, ideal for selling both wings.

SPY -0.05% / QQQ -0.42% — indices flat-to-lower and range-bound; breadth firm ($S5TW 61%) but no clear directional thrust.

  • Pre-set a profit target (~50% of max credit) and a defensive exit if price breaches either short strike.
