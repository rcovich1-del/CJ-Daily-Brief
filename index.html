Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Bull Diagonal

Buy a longer-dated in-the-money call and sell a shorter-dated out-of-the-money call against it, financing the long leg while keeping defined upside.

Best on a moderately bullish, trending underlying with low-to-moderate implied volatility.

DTE: 30–60 DTE (long leg) IV Rank: IV Rank < 50% preferred Bull Diag

Why Tonight

Tonight's normal regime — VIX 15.03, SPY above its 20- and 50-day averages, and 68% of the S&P above the 50-day — is a clean backdrop for a defined-risk bullish diagonal. Low near-term vol (VXST 11.2) keeps long-leg time value cheap while the front-month short call still collects usable premium.

VIX at 15.03 (moderate zone) with 9-day VXST at 11.2 signals low near-term fear — favorable for owning longer-dated call time value before any volatility pickup.

SPY (+0.85%) and QQQ (+1.66%) closed near record highs with Technology and Consumer Discretionary leading — a supportive uptrend for bullish diagonals.
