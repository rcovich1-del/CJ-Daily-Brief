Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Credit

Bull Put Spread

A Bull Put Spread sells a higher-strike put and buys a lower-strike put at the same expiration, collecting a net credit that is kept in full if the stock closes above the short strike at expiration.

Ideal when a stock has elevated IV Rank (>40%), a bullish or neutral directional bias, is trending above its key moving averages, and no earnings event falls within the trade window.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank >40% preferred BPS

Why Tonight

The normal regime — VIX below 18 with SPY above its 20-day SMA — is the textbook environment for defined-risk credit strategies. Friday's close marked the ninth straight weekly advance and an all-time high for the S&P 500, with breadth supported by 52% of SPX names above their 50-day MA. The screeners produced multiple 100% IVR candidates driven by the week's AI earnings momentum — exactly the elevated premium environment where BPS structuring is at its most efficient.

VIX 15.32 — Moderate Zone. Normal contango confirmed: VXST 12.59 < VXV 18.66 < VXMT 21.60. No near-term vol spike signal. Credit-selling premium is compressed but consistent — size to standard allocation.

SPY +2.32% above 20D MA, +7.51% above 50D MA — firmly above all key trend filters. QQQ +4.13% above 20D, +13.08% above 50D. AI-driven momentum intact. S&P 500 closed at record 7,580.06 — ninth straight weekly gain.
