7 Strategy in Focus — Bull Put Spread

Chosen for the regime (Normal, moderate VIX, SPY above its 20/50/200-day) and weekday rotation. A neutral-to-bullish, defined-risk credit spread.

How it works (delta / % terms)

A bull put spread sells a put and buys a further out-of-the-money put in the same expiration for a net credit.
  • Sell the ~30-delta put (roughly 5–7% below spot)
  • Buy the ~15-delta put (roughly 10% below spot)
  • Same expiration, 30–45 DTE
  • Target credit ≈ 25–35% of the width
Max profit = the credit, kept if price stays above the short put at expiry. Breakeven = short strike − credit. Time decay and any drop in implied volatility work in your favor.

Main risk

The position carries defined but asymmetric risk: the most you can lose is the spread width minus the credit received — several times the credit collected. That loss is realized if the underlying closes below the long strike at expiration, and an overnight gap (earnings, headline, or a geopolitical shock like the current oil/Iran backdrop) can jump the position past both strikes before you can adjust. In today's low index-vol setting the credit is thinner than usual, so the reward for the same tail risk is smaller — size small, prefer names without a catalyst inside the tenor, and pre-define an exit (e.g. close near 50% of max profit or if the short strike is breached).
