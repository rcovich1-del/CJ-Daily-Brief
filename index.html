Strategy in Focus
Bullish · Defined Risk Debit

Long Call

A long call is the simplest bullish defined-risk debit strategy. You buy a single call option, paying premium upfront for the right (not obligation) to own shares at the strike price by expiration.

Best deployed when you have a directional bullish thesis, IV rank is moderate-to-low (<50%), and you want uncapped upside with capped downside. Tuesday in a Normal regime triggers this as the weekly SIF rotation.

DTE: 30–45 DTE IV Rank: IV Rank <50% LC

Why Tonight

Normal regime (VIX 15–20) with improving breadth creates the ideal backdrop for directional debit trades. When VIX is moderate and trend is intact, paying debit for defined upside is capital-efficient.

VIX 17.01 — Moderate Zone. IV not cheap enough to go naked long premium carelessly, but structured calls at 30–45 DTE capture theta decay slowly enough to give the trade room.

SPY +2.35% above 20D MA, 59% breadth expanding — trend is constructive. Long call aligns with primary market direction.
